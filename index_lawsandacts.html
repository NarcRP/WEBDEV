Laws and Acts

Here is a list of some of the most common laws and their respective acts that are broken within the city. We ask that you memorise all of the acts shown below in bold and have a rough idea of what law comes under what act.

Criminal Justice Act 1988 - Revised:
  • Possession of a bladed article. [Most Common Charge]

    • Misuse Of Drugs Act 2004 - Revised:
  • Possession with intent supply [5g Or More]
  • Possession of a illegal substance [less than 5g]
  • Possession of a class A substance [Cocaine // Heroin]
  • Possession of a class B substance [Cannabis]

    • Offences Against The Persons Act 1861 - Revised:
  • Threat to life [Death or Murder]
  • Bodily harm [Assault Civilian or LEO]

    • Homicide Act 1967 - Revised:
  • 1st Degree murder [Premediatated]
  • 2nd Degree murder
  • 3rd Degree murder
  • Soliciting Murder [Complicity]

    • Road Traffic Act 1988 - Revised:
  • Vehicular manslaughter
  • Dangerous Driving
  • Driving while imbricated
  • Careless or inconsiderate driving
  • Causing serious injury via vehicle
  • Theft of a motor vehicle
  • All other traffic infractions

    • Taking Hostage Act 1982 - Revised:
  • Hostage taking of an LEO
  • Hostage taking of a Civilian
  • Attempted hostage taking of an LEO [PREVENTED]
  • Attempted hostage taking of a Civilian [PREVENTED]

    • Theft Act 1968 - Revised:
  • Theft of property [Other owned items]
  • Robbery of property [Other owned items]
  • Fraud or blackmail
  • Transaction of money through deceptions
  • Suppression Of Information
  • Aggravted vehicle taking
  • Robbery of a store
  • Robbery of a bank
  • Armed robbery of a store
  • Armed robbery of a bank

    • Policing And Crime Act 2017 - Revised:
  • Failure to comply with a lawful order

    • Criminal Attempts Act 1981 - Revised:
  • Attempted murder upon an LEO [In unison with the Homicide Act 1967]
  • Attempted murder upon a civilian [In unison with the Homicide Act 1967]

    • Proceeds Of Crime Act 2002 - Revised:
  • Possession of property seized from robbery
  • Possession of money seized from robbery

    • In addition to all of the above acts there is also the PACE act, this is one of the most crucial acts as it gives you the power to search people and premises, in particular section 32 and section 1 as you will need to use this day in day out when on duty as a psco or higher.
